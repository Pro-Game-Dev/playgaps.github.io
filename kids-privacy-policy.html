Kids Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 12, 2026

The short version

  • We don't collect your child's name, photos, voice, location, or any personal information — there's nothing to type or upload in any Play Gaps game.
  • Ads are non-personalized and child-safe, shown through Google AdMob with child-directed settings switched on for every game.
  • Some games offer optional purchases (like removing ads), handled entirely by Google Play — we never see your payment details.
  • Every Play Gaps game is part of Google Play's Designed for Families program and follows children's privacy law (COPPA).

Have a question anyway? Email contact@playgaps.com — we read every message ourselves.

1. Who This Policy Covers

This Kids Privacy Policy applies to every educational game published under the Play Gaps name on Google Play — our full library of 16 games today, plus any future Play Gaps game built for children. It is written specifically to meet Google Play's Designed for Families requirements and the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), including the amendments that reached full compliance in April 2026.

If Play Gaps ever publishes an app meant for a general, non-child audience, that app will link to a separate privacy policy written for that audience instead of this one.

2. Our Approach, in One Sentence

Every Play Gaps game is built so a child can play, learn, and have fun without ever being asked for — or needing to hand over — any personal information at all.

3. Information We Do Not Collect

There is no sign-up, no login, and no way to type anything personal into a Play Gaps game. Specifically, we do not collect, request, or store:

  • Your child's name, nickname, email address, or phone number
  • Photos, videos, voice recordings, or any biometric data (fingerprints, or face or voice patterns)
  • Precise or approximate location
  • Contacts, files, or anything else from the device
  • Any account, password, or persistent user profile

Every game is designed to be picked up and played immediately — no form to fill in, nothing to create an account for.

4. Information Collected Automatically

The only way any technical information is processed is through Google AdMob, which we use to show ads and keep our games free. AdMob's own software, running inside the game, may automatically process a small amount of technical data on Google's servers — for example, general device type and IP address — purely to serve an ad, detect fraud, and prevent the same ad from repeating too often.

We rely on COPPA's "support for internal operations" exception for this: the data is used only for those internal purposes, never to build a profile of your child, track them across other apps or websites, or contact them individually. Because every Play Gaps game is configured for child-directed advertising, current Google Play policy also blocks the advertising identifier itself from being sent along with these ad requests.

5. Advertising in Our Games

Every Play Gaps game is tagged as child-directed, and every ad request is capped at Google's "G" (General Audiences) content rating. In practice, that means:

  • Ads are contextual and non-personalized — never chosen based on a child's behavior, interests, or profile
  • We never show remarketing or behavioral advertising to children
  • We only use ad partners certified for Google Play's Families program

You can read how Google itself handles this limited advertising data in Google's Ads Policy and Google's Privacy Policy.

6. In-App Purchases

Some Play Gaps games offer optional in-app purchases — for example, removing ads. Every purchase is handled entirely by Google Play's own billing system. Play Gaps never receives, sees, or stores any payment card, bank, or financial information — that part of the transaction happens entirely within Google Play, governed by Google's own policies. Google Play requires you to confirm each purchase with your account password, PIN, or device biometrics, which helps ensure purchases are completed by a parent, not a child.

7. Third-Party Services We Use

We keep this list short on purpose. Play Gaps games use exactly one third-party service:

  • Google AdMob (advertising) and Google Play Billing (in-app purchases) — both part of Google. See Google's Privacy Policy.

We do not use Firebase, Unity Analytics, Google Play Games Services, social login, or any other analytics, tracking, or advertising SDK in any Play Gaps game.

8. Data Retention

Because we do not collect personal information from children in the first place, there is nothing of that kind for us to store or delete. Play Gaps does not operate a server or database of children's personal information. The limited technical data described in Section 4 is processed and retained according to Google's own retention rules, not ours — see Google's Privacy Policy for details.

9. Your Rights as a Parent or Guardian

You are always welcome to:

  • Ask us to confirm what information (if any) we hold about your child — as described above, the answer is none beyond the limited, non-identifying technical data used to serve ads
  • Ask a question about how a specific game works, or how it handles ads or purchases
  • Withdraw your child's use of a game at any time, simply by uninstalling it
  • Raise any concern directly with us

Play Gaps is a small, independently-run studio — when you email us, you're reaching the actual developer, not a support queue.

10. Children's Privacy Outside the United States

Google Play distributes Play Gaps games worldwide, so briefly:

  • European Union (GDPR) and United Kingdom (Children's Code): we apply the same no-profiling, data-minimizing approach described in this policy to every user, everywhere, regardless of location or age.
  • India (DPDP Act, 2023): India's specific rules for children's data are being phased in through 2027. Play Gaps already does not collect personal data from any child anywhere, which aligns with the Act's direction ahead of its enforcement date.

11. Security

The limited technical information described in this policy is processed by Google's advertising and billing infrastructure, which maintains its own industry-standard security safeguards. On our side, Play Gaps does not operate any server or database storing children's information — removing an entire category of risk by design, not by promise.

12. Changes to This Policy

If we make a meaningful change — for instance, if a future game or feature changes what information is processed — we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page and post the revised policy here. We recommend checking back occasionally.

13. Contact Us

Questions, concerns, or feedback about children's privacy in any Play Gaps game are always welcome:

Email: contact@playgaps.com

We aim to respond within a few business days.
